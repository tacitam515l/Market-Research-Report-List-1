<p><p>Mask making machines are categorized into fully automatic and semi-automatic systems based on their operational capabilities. Fully automatic machines perform all processes, from material feeding to mask packaging, with minimal human intervention, enhancing efficiency and output. In contrast, semi-automatic machines require some manual steps, such as loading or unloading materials, providing a balance between automation and operator involvement. Both types cater to varying production needs, with fully automatic machines suited for high-volume production and semi-automatic machines offering flexibility for smaller batches.</p></p>
